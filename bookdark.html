Catcher in the Rye
J. D. Salinger

About the author

Jerome David Salinger was an American author best known for his 1951 novel The Catcher in the Rye.
He was on the January 1, 1919 and died on the January 27, 2010

Summary

Holden Caulfield, expelled from Pencey Prep, escapes to New York City. Feeling lonely and disillusioned, he seeks connections but faces disappointment. He meets with old friends, but conflicts arise due to his immaturity. He briefly stays with his sister Phoebe, sharing his desire to protect innocence. Misunderstandings lead him to a teacher's home and later Grand Central Station. Eventually, Holden watches Phoebe on a carousel, realizing his longing to preserve childhood innocence.
The story ends with Holden recovering in a sanatorium, preparing for a new school
